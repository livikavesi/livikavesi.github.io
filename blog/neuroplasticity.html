Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity I would say is more commonly known as muscle memory. Let’s say you are doing weight training. The more repetition you do the easier it gets in time. The muscle doesn’t actually remembers, your brain does by connecting and lighting up certain neuro pathways. Each time you do the same exercise, the same neuro pathway is lit. The more repetition the stronger these neuro pathways get. Yes, the muscle gets stronger but only because the brain is getting more efficient, and more adept at using these pathways to send singles to the muscle and the rest of the body.

Mindset

These neurons don’t just store weight lifting information. It stores everything from your personality, emotions, memories etc. Which means, just like our muscles, our mental and emotional capability can get stronger also. Lets talk about our learning mindset.

“Mindset” written by Ph.D. Psychologist Carol S Dweck. The book talks about two different mindsets people have. Growth-mindset and Fix-mindset. A growth-mindset person believes new skills can be acquired through hard work, while a fix-mindset believes that abilities are limited. When we face a challenging situation, like a difficult code exercise or presenting to an audience. Do my inner thoughts help me push through this obstacle or does it tell me its beyond my capabilities.

Having these mindsets has a physical effect on the brain. Our brain changes, adapt based on our habits and beliefs. This is ‘Neuoplasticity’.
